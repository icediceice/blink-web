Privacy Policy

Effective March 3, 2026  ·  Last updated March 3, 2026

Your data never leaves your device.

Blink is a local-only app. We collect nothing, store nothing remotely, and share nothing with any third party. The sections below explain exactly which system permissions we use and why — so you know precisely what the app can and cannot see.

01

Overview

Blink ("we", "the app") is a personal-privacy and access-management application for Android. It detects when a protected app enters the foreground, displays a lock overlay, and requires fingerprint or PIN authentication to resume access. It runs discreetly in the background to keep your private apps private.

This Privacy Policy describes every Android permission Blink requests, why each is necessary, and how the information those permissions expose is used — entirely on your device, with no data ever leaving your phone.

02

Permissions and How We Use Them

Blink requests the following Android permissions. Each is used solely for the stated purpose. No data accessed through these permissions ever leaves your device or is shared with any third party.

Accessibility Service
Detects when a protected app enters the foreground (Advanced Mode) so Blink can display a lock overlay instantly. Blink reads window package names only — never on-screen text, keystrokes, or the content of other apps.
Usage Stats Access
Polls the foreground app name in Standard Mode when the Accessibility Service is not enabled. No usage history, patterns, or session data is recorded or transmitted.
Display Over Other Apps
Required to show a full-screen lock overlay when a protected app is detected. The overlay is a solid dim — it displays no content from the underlying app.
Notification Access
Suppresses or replaces heads-up notifications from protected apps while Blink is in an inactive session. Notification content is read briefly in memory and is never written to storage or transmitted.
Biometrics
Used for optional fingerprint authentication via Android's BiometricPrompt API. Blink never receives raw biometric data — matching happens entirely inside the Android operating system.
PIN Storage
PINs are hashed on-device using PBKDF2-HMAC-SHA-256 (310,000 iterations) and stored in Android EncryptedSharedPreferences. The plaintext PIN is never stored, logged, or transmitted.
Device Administrator
Allows Blink to lock the screen via Android's Device Policy API. Blink uses force-lock only — it does not have permission to wipe device data or perform a factory reset.
Receive Boot Completed
Starts the Blink foreground service at device boot so app protection is active before the user unlocks. No personal data is collected or processed at boot.
Foreground Service
Keeps Blink running persistently in the background. Android requires a visible notification for foreground services — this notification confirms Blink is active.
Volume Key Events
Detects the 5-button volume sequence used to open a Blink session. The sequence is processed in memory only and is never logged or stored.
03

What Blink Does Not Do

These are hard commitments, not aspirations.

  • No data is collected, stored remotely, or transmitted off your device.
  • No analytics, telemetry, crash-reporting, or advertising SDKs are included.
  • No third party ever receives any information about you or your usage.
  • Blink does not read, record, or store the content of your messages or emails.
  • Blink does not access your contacts, call log, or location.
  • Blink does not use your camera or microphone.
  • Blink does not upload your PIN hash, biometric reference, or any credential.
  • Blink does not perform factory resets or wipe device data.
04

Local Data Storage

Blink stores your configuration — protected apps, intercept mode, session timeout, and PIN hash — in Android EncryptedSharedPreferences, an on-device encrypted key-value store backed by the Android Keystore hardware module. This data is inaccessible to other apps and is not included in unencrypted Android backups.

All encryption keys are generated on your device and are never exported. Uninstalling Blink permanently removes all stored data.

A short diagnostic log — up to 20 intercept event timestamps, no app names or personal data — is held in memory during the current session only and discarded when the app is terminated.
